Relationship Stress vs Academic Performance

This study investigates the correlation between stress levels and academic performance among undergraduate students. It explores how chronic stress, time pressure, and emotional fatigue impact cognitive functioning and exam outcomes. By analyzing survey data and academic records, the research aims to identify patterns and potential risk factors. The study also considers the role of support systems, such as peer networks and campus counseling services, in mitigating stress.

Post-Pandemic Coping Mechanisms for the Youth.

This research explores the coping strategies adopted by youth in the aftermath of the COVID-19 pandemic. It examines how young people navigate emotional distress, social isolation, and academic disruptions through mechanisms such as mindfulness, peer support, digital communities, and creative expression. The study draws on qualitative interviews and psychological assessments to identify patterns of resilience and vulnerability.

Early Childhood Intervention & Psychological Resilience.

This study examines how early childhood interventions contribute to the development of psychological resilience later in life. It focuses on programs that support emotional regulation, secure attachment, and cognitive stimulation during formative years. By analyzing longitudinal data and case studies, the research highlights how timely support can buffer against future mental health challenges and enhance adaptive coping skills. The study also explores the role of caregivers, educators, and community resources in fostering resilience.
